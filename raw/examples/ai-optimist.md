# AI Will Create More Jobs Than It Destroys

## Author: Dr. Sarah Chen, MIT Economics Department, 2025

## Executive Summary

Contrary to widespread fears, historical evidence and current data strongly suggest that AI will be a net positive for employment. While certain jobs will be displaced, AI will create entirely new categories of work that we cannot yet imagine — just as the internet did.

## Key Arguments

### 1. Historical Precedent

Every major technological revolution has ultimately created more jobs than it destroyed:
- The Industrial Revolution eliminated millions of agricultural jobs but created the entire manufacturing sector
- The automobile killed the horse-and-buggy industry but spawned gas stations, highways, motels, and suburbs
- The internet eliminated travel agents and video stores but created social media managers, app developers, and e-commerce specialists

**AI will follow the same pattern.**

### 2. Current Data

According to the World Economic Forum's 2025 Future of Jobs Report:
- AI is expected to **create 97 million new jobs** globally by 2028
- Only 85 million jobs are expected to be displaced
- Net positive: **+12 million jobs**

### 3. New Job Categories Already Emerging

- **Prompt Engineers** — Annual salary: $120K-$300K
- **AI Safety Researchers** — Demand up 400% since 2023
- **AI Ethics Officers** — Now required in EU companies with >500 employees
- **Human-AI Collaboration Designers** — Designing workflows where humans and AI work together
- **AI Training Data Curators** — Ensuring high-quality, unbiased training data

### 4. Productivity Boost = Economic Growth = More Jobs

AI increases productivity by an estimated 25-40% in knowledge work. Higher productivity leads to:
- Lower costs → Lower prices → Increased demand → More jobs
- Higher profits → More investment → New ventures → More jobs
- GDP growth of 7-10% over the next decade (McKinsey estimate)

## Conclusion

**The fear of AI unemployment is historically unfounded.** We should focus not on preventing AI adoption, but on ensuring smooth transitions — retraining programs, education reform, and social safety nets.

> "The best way to predict the future is to invent it." — Alan Kay
